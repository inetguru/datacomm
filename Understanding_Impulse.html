Periodic Rectangular Pulse and Fourier Coefficients

Pulse amplitude = 1/D, pulse width = D, period = P. The pulse is centered at each integer multiple of P, so one period has value 1/D on [-D/2, D/2] and 0 elsewhere.

Complex Fourier series coefficient
\( c_k = \frac{1}{P}\int_{-D/2}^{D/2}\frac{1}{D}e^{-jk\omega_0 t}\,dt \)
\( \omega_0 = \frac{2\pi}{P} \)
\( c_k = \begin{cases} \frac{1}{P}, & k=0 \\ \frac{\sin(\pi k D/P)}{\pi k D}, & k\neq 0 \end{cases} \)
Equivalent form: \( c_k = \frac{1}{P}\,\mathrm{sinc}\!\left(\frac{kD}{P}\right) \), where \( \mathrm{sinc}(x)=\frac{\sin(\pi x)}{\pi x} \)
