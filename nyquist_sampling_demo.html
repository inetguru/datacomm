Key Concepts
🎯
Nyquist Theorem
To perfectly reconstruct an analog signal from its digital samples, the sampling frequency (fs) must be at least twice the highest frequency (fmax) present in the signal.

fs ≥ 2 × fmax
👻
Aliasing
When the condition is violated, phantom low-frequency signals appear that were not in the original. This distortion is called aliasing.

Example: CD audio (44.1 kHz) is designed to exceed twice the human hearing limit (20 kHz).
🔬
Real-World Applications
CD Audio: 44,100 Hz sampling
Phone Voice: 8,000 Hz sampling
Medical MRI: k-space sampling
Digital Camera: anti-aliasing filter

Applied across all digital signal processing.
