What is AM?
Amplitude Modulation multiplies a high-frequency carrier signal by a slowly-varying envelope. The result is a wave whose amplitude varies over time according to the modulator.
y = sin(2π f₁ t) × (1 + A₂ cos(2π f₂ t))
Sidebands
Using a trig identity, the AM signal can be rewritten as a sum of three sinusoids:
= sin(2π f₁ t)
+ (A₂/2) sin(2π(f₁+f₂)t)
+ (A₂/2) sin(2π(f₁−f₂)t)
The two extra tones at f₁ ± f₂ are called sidebands.
What you hear
When f₂ is very low (< 20 Hz), you hear a single note whose loudness trembles at rate f₂.

When f₂ grows above ~20 Hz, the sidebands become audible and you hear two separate tones — like a dial tone.
